What is waste and how does it affects us?

Waste harms human health, the environment, and economy through pollution, toxic exposure, and climate change. Improper disposal contaminates air, water, and soil, causing respiratory issues and cancer. It also destroys ecosystems, harms biodiversity, and impacts economies. Sustainable practices like reducing, reusing, recycling, and proper disposal can create a healthier environment for generation to come. Waste poses a significant threat to environmental sustainability and global health. The accumulationof waste, particular plastics, electronics and hazardous materials, contributes to pollution, climate change and loss of biodiversity. Human activities, including consumerism and unsustainable practices, have resulted in the generation of approximately 2 billion tons of waste annually. Improper dssposal of waste exacerbates environmental desgradation, contaminating oceans, landfils and public spaces. To mitigate these issues, adopting a circular economy, reducing single-use plastics, recycling efficiently and developing innovative waste-to-energy technologies are crucial steps. individuals can contribute by minimizing waste generation, composting organic materials and supporting community initiatives focused on sustainable waste management. collective action and responsible waste management practices are essential for mitigating environmental harm and promoting ecological balance.

The 3Rs of waste management

Reduce

Lowering the amount of waste generated by consuming less or choosing more sustainable options.

Reuse

Extending the life of items by using them again, repurposing them, or finding alternative uses instead of discarding them.

Recycle

Processing materials to create new products, this preventig them from going to landfills and conserving natural resources.
